about

I am a PhD candidate in the department of biostatistics at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health. My advisor is Jeff Leek.

I'm working in computational biology, developing statistical methods and software for detecting differential gene expression using RNA sequencing data. I've published a few papers in the area.

I was in the Summer 2013 batch of Hacker School and loved it. I've written several blog posts, including two that were fairly popular: Introducing R to a non-programmer in one hour and Exploring the data on gender and GitHub repo ownership.

If you want to get in touch: I'm alyssa.frazee at gmail, @acfrazee on Twitter, and alyssafrazee on GitHub.
