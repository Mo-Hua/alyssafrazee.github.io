mac music management minus iTunes

Wed 11 December 2013 | -- (permalink)

I hadn't been totally thrilled with the last several versions of iTunes for Mac, but everything was going smoothly for me until one day, a couple of months ago, when I encountered a really weird issue. I was coding in my shared office when, all of a sudden, Fanfare for the Common Man started blaring out of my laptop speakers. Remember: shared office. Awkward. I quickly paused the music, closed iTunes, and apologized to my office mates. Luckily they're cool. Back to work.

But then, five seconds later, same scenario. Whaaaa? I closed iTunes five seconds ago. And here comes Fanfare for the Common Man! Enough! I mute my computer and quite iTunes again. This time, very definitively. Whatever that means.

Five more seconds. Same scenario. This time I literally see the iTunes icon pop up in my dock, unprompted, and again, Fanfare for the Common Man begins to play. Muted, of course, but seriously. This calls for me to quit working for a moment and try to fix this, so I google around a bit. My findings, mainly from Apple message boards, were mainly along the lines of "some other device or app is interacting with your iTunes software. Disable it and you're good to go." Okay, fine. After getting my officemates to check their machines, shutting off my iPhone, making sure I don't have any crazy apps installed on my own computer, and checking in the offices next to me to make sure nobody was using a universal Apple remote, I conclude that I don't have this "external app/device" issue, BUT the unprompted Fanfare for the Common Man happens AGAIN.

At this point, I've had quite enough and decide to delete iTunes completely. I will play my music only when I want to play my music and not when some iTunes bug decides to act up.

Turns out Apple tries to trick you into thinking that OSX requires iTunes (i.e., you're not allowed to delete iTunes from Finder or drag the dock icon to the trash), but I ran sudo rm iTunes.app in the Applications folder. Playing around with sudo rm isn't really my jam, but I was really mad, so I took a deep breath, checked carefully for typos, and deleted iTunes.

Ahhhhh, blissful silence.
